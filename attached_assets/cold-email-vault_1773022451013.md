## Why These Work

Most cold emails fail because they're long, generic, and about the sender. Every template here follows three rules:

1. **Short** — Under 100 words. Nobody reads novels from strangers.
2. **About them** — Lead with their problem, not your resume.
3. **One clear CTA** — Don't ask for a call, a reply, AND a website visit. Pick one.

Average cold email reply rate: 1-3%. These templates are designed for 8-15%+ when personalized properly.

## 📧 SECTION 1: Freelance Client Outreach (5 Templates)

### Template 1: The Specific Observation

**Subject:** Quick thought on [their website/content/product]

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [specific observation about their business — e.g., "your landing page doesn't mention your 4.8-star Google rating" or "your last 3 blog posts haven't been promoted on LinkedIn"].

[One sentence about the missed opportunity — e.g., "Adding social proof above the fold typically lifts conversion 15-30%."]

I help [type of business] with [your service]. Would it be worth a 10-minute chat to see if I could help?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Shows you actually looked at their business. The specific observation proves competence before you even pitch.

**Personalization tip:** Spend 2 minutes on their website. Find ONE real thing to comment on. Generic = deleted.

### Template 2: The Case Study Drop

**Subject:** How I helped [similar company] get [specific result]

```
Hi [First Name],

I recently helped [similar business type] [specific result — e.g., "increase their email open rates from 18% to 34%" or "generate 47 qualified leads in 30 days"].

I think I could do something similar for [their company] because [one specific reason].

Here's the case study if you're curious: [link]

Worth a quick chat?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Social proof + specificity. Real numbers beat vague claims every time.

**Personalization tip:** If you don't have a case study, use a before/after from your own projects.

**Subject:** [Specific deliverable] for [Company Name] — on the house

## 📧 SECTION 4: Follow-Ups That Don't Annoy (5 Templates)

### Template 16: The Gentle Bump (Day 3-5)

**Subject:** Re: [original subject]

```
Hi [First Name],

Just floating this back up in case it got buried. Would love to hear your thoughts.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Short, no guilt-tripping. Most replies come from follow-ups, not first emails.

### Template 18: The Breakup Email (Day 14-21)

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

```
Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a couple of times about [your offer]. I don't want to be a pest.

Should I:
a) Set up a quick call this week
b) Check back in a few months
c) Stop reaching out

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** The "breakup" creates urgency and makes it easy to reply. Often gets the highest response rate of any follow-up.

## 🎯 The Cold Email Checklist

Before you hit send, check every box:

- [ ] **Subject line is under 6 words** (shorter = higher open rate)
- [ ] **Email is under 100 words** (respect their time)
- [ ] **First sentence is about THEM, not you** (observation, pain, or compliment)
- [ ] **Includes one specific detail** that proves you researched them
- [ ] **Has exactly ONE call-to-action** (reply, book a call, OR check a link — not all three)
- [ ] **No attachments** in the first email (triggers spam filters)
- [ ] **Sent between Tue-Thu, 8-10 AM their time zone** (highest open rates)
- [ ] **Has a plain-text feel** (no fancy HTML, images, or formatting — looks like a real human email)
- [ ] **Follow-up sequence is planned** (at least 3 follow-ups over 2-3 weeks)
