## 📧 Section 1: Cold Email Prompts (8 Prompts)

### Prompt 1 — The Pain-Point Opener
> You are a B2B cold email copywriter. Write a cold email from [YOUR NAME/COMPANY] to [PROSPECT'S ROLE] at [PROSPECT'S COMPANY]. The email should:
> - Open with a specific pain point related to [INDUSTRY PROBLEM]
> - Reference something real about their company (I'll provide: [RESEARCH NOTE])
> - Propose a clear benefit, not a feature
> - End with a low-friction CTA (no "hop on a call" — suggest something easier)
> - Keep it under 90 words
>
> Tone: confident but not pushy. Like a smart friend giving advice.

### Prompt 2 — The Case Study Teaser
> Write a cold email that leads with a mini case study. Structure:
> - Line 1: "[SIMILAR COMPANY] was struggling with [PROBLEM]"
> - Line 2: What changed (keep vague enough to create curiosity)
> - Line 3: The result — use a specific number: [METRIC, e.g., "cut response time by 60%"]
> - Line 4: Bridge to the prospect: "Noticed [PROSPECT'S COMPANY] might be facing something similar"
> - Line 5: Soft CTA
>
> Keep the entire email under 80 words. No subject line yet — I'll ask for that separately.

### Prompt 3 — The Subject Line Generator
> Generate 10 cold email subject lines for an email selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE] to [TARGET ROLE] at [COMPANY SIZE] companies. Requirements:
> - No clickbait or ALL CAPS
> - At least 3 should include personalization placeholders (e.g., {{firstName}}, {{company}})
> - Mix of curiosity-driven, benefit-driven, and question-based
> - All under 45 characters
> - Rank them from most likely to get opened to least, and explain your reasoning for the top 3

### Prompt 4 — The Follow-Up Sequence (3 emails)
> Write a 3-email follow-up sequence for a cold prospect who hasn't replied to my initial outreach. Context:
> - My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
> - What we offer: [SERVICE/PRODUCT]
> - Initial email angle: [BRIEF SUMMARY]
>
> Rules:
> - Email 2 (sent Day 3): Add new value — a relevant insight, stat, or resource. Don't just "bump"
> - Email 3 (sent Day 7): Create gentle urgency or change the angle entirely
> - Email 4 (sent Day 14): The "breakup email" — give them an easy out
> - Each email should be under 60 words
> - Never guilt-trip or say "just following up"

### Prompt 5 — The "I Noticed Something" Email
> Write a cold email that opens by referencing something specific I noticed about the prospect's business. I'll provide:
> - Prospect company: [COMPANY]
> - What I noticed: [OBSERVATION — e.g., "their careers page has 5 open sales roles", "they just raised a Series B", "their website doesn't have a chatbot"]
> - My offer: [WHAT YOU DO]
>
> The email should connect the observation to a problem, then position my offer as the solution. Keep it under 85 words. Make the observation feel like genuine research, not a trick.

### Prompt 6 — The Warm Referral Request
> Write an email I can send to [MUTUAL CONNECTION'S NAME] asking for an introduction to [PROSPECT NAME] at [PROSPECT COMPANY]. The email should:
> - Remind them briefly how we know each other: [CONTEXT]
> - Explain in one sentence why I want to connect with the prospect
> - Make it effortless for them to forward (include a blurb they can copy-paste)
> - Be warm and grateful, not transactional
> - Under 100 words

### Prompt 7 — The Value-First Email (Give Before You Ask)
> Write a cold email where I lead with free value instead of a pitch. Structure:
> - I've prepared [FREE RESOURCE — e.g., "a quick audit of your landing page", "3 ideas to improve your onboarding flow", "a competitive analysis of your space"]
> - Deliver the value upfront in 2-3 bullet points (I'll provide: [KEY INSIGHTS])
> - Then a single soft line: "Happy to walk through the details if useful — no strings"
>
> Target: [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. Keep it under 100 words. The email should feel like a gift, not a pitch.

### Prompt 8 — The Reactivation Email
> Write a cold email to a prospect who went cold after initially showing interest. Context:
> - They previously: [WHAT THEY DID — e.g., "replied positively but ghosted after", "booked a call then no-showed", "downloaded our resource"]
> - Time since last contact: [TIMEFRAME]
> - My offer: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
>
> The email should acknowledge the gap without being passive-aggressive. Offer a fresh reason to re-engage (new feature, case study, or market change). Under 70 words.

## 💼 Section 2: LinkedIn Outreach Prompts (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 9 — The Connection Request Note
> Write a LinkedIn connection request message (under 300 characters!) to [PROSPECT ROLE] at [COMPANY]. Context:
> - Why I want to connect: [REASON]
> - Something we have in common: [SHARED INTEREST, MUTUAL CONNECTION, SAME INDUSTRY, etc.]
>
> Rules: No selling. No pitching. Just a genuine, human reason to connect. Make it feel like two professionals meeting at a conference, not a sales ambush.

### Prompt 10 — The Post-Connection First Message
> I just connected with [NAME], a [ROLE] at [COMPANY] on LinkedIn. Write my first DM. It should:
> - Reference their recent activity if I provide it: [RECENT POST/ARTICLE/JOB CHANGE]
> - Ask a genuine question about their work or perspective
> - NOT pitch anything
> - Plant a seed for future conversation about [MY TOPIC AREA]
> - Be under 75 words and conversational

### Prompt 11 — The LinkedIn Voice Note Script
> Write a 30-second voice note script I can send via LinkedIn to [PROSPECT NAME], a [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. The script should:
> - Sound natural and unscripted (include filler words like "honestly" or "actually" sparingly)
> - Mention one specific thing about them or their company
> - Make a single clear ask
> - Feel like I'm talking to a human, not reading a pitch deck
>
> My offer: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Keep it to 60-80 words spoken.

### Prompt 12 — The Comment-to-DM Pipeline
> I want to engage with [PROSPECT NAME]'s LinkedIn content before DMing them. Provide:
> 1. A thoughtful comment (2-3 sentences) I can leave on their recent post about [TOPIC]. The comment should add value, not just say "Great post!"
> 2. A follow-up DM to send 2-3 days later that references my comment and opens a conversation. Under 60 words
> 3. A second DM for 5 days later if they don't reply. Change the angle. Under 50 words
>
> Goal: Build familiarity before pitching [MY OFFER]

### Prompt 13 — The LinkedIn Mutual Connection Intro
> Write a LinkedIn message asking [MUTUAL CONNECTION] to introduce me to [PROSPECT]. Format:
> - Line 1: Quick personal touch (reference last interaction or shared experience)
> - Line 2: Who I want to meet and why (one sentence)
> - Line 3: A forwarded blurb they can send directly to the prospect
> - Line 4: Thank them and make it easy to say no
>
> Total message: under 100 words. The forwardable blurb: under 40 words.

## 🔁 Section 3: Bonus Prompts — Strategy & Optimization (2 Prompts)

### Prompt 14 — The ICP Research Prompt
> I'm building a cold outreach campaign for [MY PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Help me define my Ideal Customer Profile. Ask me 10 questions about my business, then based on my answers, output:
> 1. A detailed ICP description (industry, company size, role, pain points, buying triggers)
> 2. 5 specific companies that match this profile (with reasoning)
> 3. The top 3 objections this ICP will have, and how to preempt them in my outreach
> 4. Recommended outreach channel (cold email vs. LinkedIn vs. both) with reasoning

### Prompt 15 — The A/B Test Generator
> I have a cold email that's getting [CURRENT OPEN/REPLY RATE]. Here's the email:
>
> [PASTE YOUR CURRENT EMAIL]
>
> Generate 3 variations to A/B test:
> - Variation A: Different opening line (change the hook)
> - Variation B: Different CTA (change the ask)
> - Variation C: Different angle entirely (reframe the value prop)
>
> For each variation, explain what you changed and why it might perform better. Also suggest what metric to track for each test.

## 💡 Usage Tips

### Getting the Best Results
1. **Fill in every bracket.** The more specific your inputs, the better the output. "[RESEARCH NOTE]" should be a real observation — check their LinkedIn, website, or recent news.
2. **Don't send the first draft.** These prompts generate strong starting points. Read the output aloud. Does it sound like something a human would actually say? Edit until it does.
3. **Personalize at scale.** Use Prompt 14 (ICP Research) first to nail your targeting. Then batch-generate emails with Prompts 1-8. Personalize the first line of each one manually — that's where the magic is.
4. **Test everything.** Use Prompt 15 regularly. Small changes to subject lines and CTAs can 2-3x your reply rates.
5. **Respect the inbox.** These prompts are designed for relevance, not volume. Send 20 great emails, not 200 generic ones.

### Recommended Stack
- **Email sending:** Instantly, Lemlist, or Smartlead (all have free trials)
- **Finding emails:** Apollo.io, Hunter.io (free tiers available)
- **LinkedIn automation:** Do it manually for the first 50 — you'll learn what works before automating
- **AI model:** Works with ChatGPT (GPT-4), Claude, Gemini, or any capable LLM

### The 5-Day Quick Start
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Run Prompt 14 — define your ICP |
| 2 | Research 20 prospects. Use Prompt 5 or 7 to write personalized emails |
| 3 | Send first batch. Write LinkedIn connection notes with Prompt 9 |
| 4 | Follow up on LinkedIn connections with Prompt 10. Monitor email opens |
| 5 | Use Prompt 15 to optimize. Send second batch with improvements |
